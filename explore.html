Mission-to-spec software for autonomous machine teams

Stop building the wrong machine before you've cut a single part.

Sture Explore turns a mission brief into a review-ready system specification, citing every standards, safety, or threshold claim it makes to a real source, and flagging anything it can't prove as an assumption for your team to approve. A first defensible draft in hours, not weeks.

For teams designing autonomous drones, rovers, and surface vessels. Opens 1 August 2026.

The cheapest place to be wrong is a sentence.

You can change a sentence for free. You cannot change a prototype, a carbon-fibre airframe, or a 42-week-lead-time MCU for free.

Yet most autonomous-machine programs discover the mission was infeasible after the aluminium is already cut, when the endurance won't support the payload, the thermal budget was fantasy, or the autonomy stack ate twice the compute anyone assumed. Everyone did good work. None of it mattered, because the mission itself was over-constrained from day one.

Explore moves that argument to where it is still cheap: the specification.

What Explore does.

  • 01Define the mission.

    Explore interviews you, drafts a sharp problem definition, and lets you edit and approve it before anything else happens.

  • 02Compare real options.

    Three genuinely different concepts, with assets, engineering notes, and a trade-off matrix. Steer them, then lock the one that fits.

  • 03Build the specification.

    A full draft system specification, section by section, editable, with change requests and approval gates.

  • 04Cite the evidence.

    Each standards, safety, or threshold statement is cited to a real source, down to the page, or flagged plainly as an assumption. No invented citations.

  • 05End with a decision.

    A clear go/no-go readiness call: what is ready, what is not, and the single next action.

Most tools generate confident text. Explore generates text you can defend.

Every claim about a standard, a safety threshold, or a regulatory requirement carries its source. Anything it cannot back, it tells you is an assumption, not a fact. So when a regulator, a customer's engineering team, an investor's diligence process, or a crash investigation finally asks "why did you build it this way," the answer already exists, instead of living in a Slack thread from March.

If you can't trace it, you can't trust it. That is the whole product.

Where Explore ends.

Explore takes you from a problem to an approved, evidence-backed specification. The full engine, design through manufacture, test, compliance, and telemetry, follows in Sture Complete.
