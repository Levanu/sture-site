Spec-to-field software for autonomous machine teams

Launches 8 November 2026

Explore gets you the right spec. Complete keeps the whole machine aligned while your team builds it.

Sture Complete is the integration and validation layer for autonomous-machine programs. It keeps architecture, electronics, mechanical design, firmware, compliance, sourcing, test, and field evidence in sync as the design changes. Every change is checked across the machine, conflicts are surfaced early, your engineers approve every decision, and the reasoning travels with the work.

8 November is the birthday of Sture, the grandfather this company is named for.

From spec to field, without losing the thread.

Autonomous-machine teams don't fail because one discipline did bad work. They fail when the disciplines drift apart.

  • A payload change breaks endurance.
  • A board change breaks thermal.
  • A compute decision breaks autonomy.
  • A sourcing substitution breaks compliance.
  • A field result contradicts the model.

Complete catches those conflicts while they are still cheap to fix.

Architecture and design

  • Drafts architecture trade studies for engineer review: vehicle class, power, propulsion, sensing, compute, comms, and safety.
  • Proposes detailed-design updates and checks they stay consistent across schematic, PCB, harness, BOM, and the mechanical build.

Validation and sourcing

  • Runs reviewable analyses across power, endurance, thermal, dynamics, RF, structural, vibration, and environmental constraints.
  • Surfaces sourcing reality: parts, alternates, lead time, cost, availability, and local supply chain.

Software, test, and compliance

  • Scaffolds firmware and autonomy work for your engineers to own: ROS 2, PX4, Nav2, MAVLink, RTOS, and embedded Linux.
  • Drafts test plans, acceptance criteria, operating procedures, compliance evidence, FMEA, hazard analysis, and operating-envelope docs.

Field loop

  • Ingests simulation, HIL, and field telemetry, then compares predicted behaviour with observed.
  • When the machine disagrees with the model, it shows the gap and proposes redesigns for engineer approval.

Traceability

  • Every output traces back to the spec, rules, corpus, and components it came from. Nothing important is a black box.

Under the hood

  • Multi-board split detection via the 14-pattern daughter-board table.
  • CAD sync: STEP and glTF export, SOLIDWORKS and PDM read, bidirectional write.
  • Reproducible, signed generation trace: spec, engine, rule, corpus, and component versions, plus artifacts and evidence bundle.
  • Canonical ReadinessProofReport as the final readiness authority.
  • Adapter and external-compute layer for long-running simulators and GPU jobs.
  • Deep air, ground, and water domain packs on the shared core.
