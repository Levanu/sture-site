Spec-to-field software for autonomous machine teams

Early access opens 8 November 2026

Explore gets you to the right specification. Complete helps keep the machine aligned as your team turns that specification into engineering work.

Sture Complete is an integration and validation layer for autonomous-machine programs. It connects the approved spec to architecture, electronics, mechanical design, firmware, sourcing, test planning, compliance evidence, and field feedback. As the design changes, Complete checks the supported machine graph, surfaces conflicts early, and keeps the reasoning attached to the work.

Your engineers stay in control. Sture proposes, checks, traces, and flags. Your team reviews and approves.

8 November is the birthday of Sture, the grandfather this company is named for.

From spec to field, without losing the thread.

Autonomous-machine programs rarely fail because one discipline did bad work. They fail because the disciplines drift apart.

  • A payload change affects endurance.
  • A board change affects thermal margin.
  • A compute decision affects autonomy.
  • A sourcing substitution affects compliance evidence.
  • A test result contradicts the model.

Complete is built to catch those gaps while they are still reviewable, traceable, and cheaper to fix.

Architecture and design

  • Drafts architecture trade studies for engineer review, including vehicle class, power, propulsion, sensing, compute, communications, and safety assumptions.
  • Proposes design updates and checks them against the supported system graph: schematic, PCB, harness, BOM, mechanical interfaces, mounting, enclosure, and operating constraints.
  • Flags cross-domain conflicts for review instead of hiding them in separate documents, spreadsheets, or tool exports.

Validation and sourcing

  • Runs reviewable analyses across the supported engineering domains: power, endurance, thermal, dynamics, RF, structural, vibration, and environmental constraints.
  • Surfaces sourcing reality where data is available: candidate parts, alternates, lead time, cost, availability, substitution risk, and local supply-chain assumptions.
  • When evidence is missing, stale, synthetic, or not yet connected to a live provider, Complete labels it plainly instead of treating it as proven.

Software, test, and compliance

  • Scaffolds firmware and autonomy work for engineer ownership across supported stacks including ROS 2, PX4, Nav2, MAVLink, RTOS, and embedded Linux workflows.
  • Drafts test plans, acceptance criteria, operating procedures, compliance evidence, FMEA, hazard analysis, and operating-envelope documentation.
  • Every generated output is intended for review. Complete does not certify the machine, approve deployment, or replace engineering sign-off.

Simulation, HIL, and field evidence

  • Ingests simulation, HIL, and field evidence where connected, and compares predicted behaviour with observed results.
  • When the machine disagrees with the model, Complete shows the gap, traces the affected assumptions, and proposes redesigns or follow-up checks for engineer approval.
  • Unsupported simulators, missing credentials, synthetic fixtures, and unavailable field data remain explicit blockers.

Traceability

  • Every important output traces back to the spec, rules, corpus, component data, assumptions, and generated artifacts that produced it.
  • Complete keeps a reproducible generation trace so reviews can answer the practical question: why did we build it this way?

Under the hood

  • Multi-board split detection using the 14-pattern daughter-board table.
  • CAD workflow support for STEP and glTF exports, SOLIDWORKS and PDM metadata reads, and reviewed writeback through supported CAD paths.
  • Signed generation traces covering spec, engine, rule, corpus, component versions, artifacts, and evidence bundles.
  • Canonical ReadinessProofReport as the readiness authority for supported workflows.
  • Adapter contracts for long-running simulator and GPU jobs, with unavailable providers blocked explicitly until configured.
  • Air, ground, and water domain packs built on the shared Sture core, with domain-specific checks, artifacts, and review gates.
